Monopoly Datacracy

2019 — A reinterpretation of Monopoly, a project carried out alongside Arthur Van Der Werf, Mattia Cittadino, Emma Hoogenboezem, and Riccardo Negretti.

We took the classic Monopoly rules and redesigned them to better represent our personal data-hungry society.

The game is a representation of the society we live in, based on the collection and use of personal data by big tech companies, and is a variant of the traditional Monopoly.

Every player, in addition to money, also owns private information. Lands are replaced by internet companies that can be bought using money. When a player lands on a company owned by other players, they have to pay with their personal data. When passing "GO", the player receives a salary based on the amount of data collected by their owned companies. If a player loses all their personal information, they are out of the game.

The game mechanics are provocative, as they highlight the importance of data over the value of money, and the companies that use them are more powerful than those who do not. The goal is to push the player to reflect on how they use their personal data given to these companies.

I took care of designing the game mechanics, creating the graphics of the board and the box.
