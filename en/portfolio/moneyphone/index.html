Moneyphone

2017 — Home Banking system created in collaboration with Nicola Brignoli, Paolo Boni, and Federico De Luca.

  • Portfolio
  • Resume
  • About

MoneyPhone was born from the desire to breathe new life into old or unused objects by rethinking their original function, while maintaining their current form.

The object we used is an old rotary phone, suitably modified to allow the insertion of coins. It counts them and stores them in a container below, not visible to the user. MoneyPhone allows the withdrawal of any amount of money (if available) and this action happens by dialing a secret PIN on the rotary. At this point, the phone will ring and the user will be required to answer. A prerecorded voice will guide the user through the various interactions.

All interactions with MoneyPhone are carried out through the rotary dial and the handset, where a recorded voice guides the user. By navigating the MoneyPhone menu, or dialing numbers associated with a choice, the user can access a variety of information about the coins inserted.

MoneyPhone is capable of:

  • — Protecting system access by requiring a PIN code, known only to the user.
  • — Counting and sorting all coins inserted under any circumstances, without the need for a PIN code.
  • — Keeping a record of the coins in memory.
  • — Reporting the available balance upon request.
  • — Dispensing the entire balance or an amount chosen by the user.
