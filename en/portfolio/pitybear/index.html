Pitybear

2017 — In collaboration with Francesca Manani, Ivana Riva, and Lucia Ferrari.

  • Portfolio
  • Resume
  • About

PityBear is an immersive experience designed for the Synthesis Laboratory. Our task was to convey a specific emotion—Pity—using a designated physical space that we tailored for the purpose. We had to craft multiple artifacts for the project, ranging from the exhibition's identity to final user interaction through storytelling, projections, and interactions. The storyline revolves around a teddy bear lost by its child owner, both of whom are trying to find each other but never quite succeed.

The centerpiece of the experience is the stylized teddy bear with a sad countenance, a small robot designed to aimlessly wander around the stand, symbolizing its search for its child owner. It features an external polystyrene shell that hides the mechanism enabling movement. I was responsible for its design, construction (hardware part), and programming, creating its movements.
